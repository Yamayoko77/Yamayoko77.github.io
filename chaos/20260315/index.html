关于黄桃的战争 / The Great Peach War

March 15, 2026

The supermarket was blasting New Year music. Three people formed a triangle around a shopping cart piled with fruit, vegetables, and red couplets, staring each other down. Between them sat the last can of yellow peaches.

The first woman was small and elderly. She wore a pink floral padded coat, with grey corduroy sleeve covers guarding her wrists. Short grey hair lay neatly behind her gleaming gold earrings. Her short legs were planted wide and steady, and her narrowed eyes shone with a fierce light that showed no sign of yielding.

The second was an old man, as thin as a pin and slightly unsteady. He wore a heavy jacket, and a small cap padded with folded newspaper covered his shiny bald head. He stood upright, muttering “the peach one” and other gibberish, casting disappointed looks at his two opponents.

The third woman was almost mountainous, draped in a gold leopard-print fur coat. She held a chubby toddler who was chewing his fingers, while a little girl in a red sweater circled nearby, unable to hold her mother’s hand and clearly worried about the situation. The woman looked down at the two elders with clear intent.

One song ended. A fast drumbeat immediately began.

The old lady’s eyes flickered. She turned to Ms. Leopard. “Dear, wouldn’t this be better suited for those of us in our twilight years? Surely someone young like you would prefer something fresh…”

The toddler protested with a sharp cry. Hearing that, Ms. Leopard frowned and reached for the jar. “You see? My kid wants it.”

The old man, who had been vibrating quietly like a concerned chicken, suddenly grew urgent. “You can’t do that—”

Nobody listened. The old lady slipped forward with surprising speed, intercepting Ms. Leopard’s hand. Ms. Leopard clicked her tongue and stepped closer, pressing directly toward the can.

The old man tried again: “My granddaughter said she wants—” Still ignored, he let out a thunderous throat-clear, circled around, and dived for the jar.

Sensing the crisis, the little girl stomped and cried, “MOMMY!”

All three adults jumped. The old man nearly left the ground, but his hand had already touched the can.

The old lady grabbed the lid. “Hey, hey, no rush!”

Ms. Leopard seized the bottom. “First come, first served!”

The poor can of peaches performed a tense ballet on its cardboard shelf. Three hands pushed and pulled it back and forth. Fingers hooked fingers. Jewelry struck jewelry. The peaches rolled anxiously in their syrup. The three adults rotated around it like a carousel, yet none would release their grip.

Then, a large, veined hand descended from above and calmly grasped the center of the can.

All three looked up.

A muscular delivery worker in a fluorescent green uniform stood before them. On his chest were the words “Supermarket Delivery” and a smiling cartoon cabbage. A stack of orders and a scanner hung at his waist. He examined the three hands, then the three faces, and turned his wrist. The can slipped free, flew in a parabola, and landed in his palm. He scanned the barcode with a beep, dropped it into a blue plastic crate, and a calm female voice announced, “Yellow peach can 1L dispatched.” Then he walked off smoothly, turning left and right through the bright red decorations and “on sale” signs, and disappeared.

The three stared at each other. The old lady’s hand remained in the air, slightly trembling. The old man’s lips moved but produced nothing. Ms. Leopard looked stunned. The toddler’s eyes filled with tears and his mouth slowly curved downward.

Just then, a trolley appeared from the other side of the shelf. A young girl in a green uniform hummed as she pushed it along. The trolley was stacked with a shining golden hill of yellow peach cans.

Wearing headphones and swaying slightly, she began stocking the shelf. Only then did she notice the three petrified adults.

“Morning?” She removed one earphone. “Everything going alright then?”

Seeing the mountain of peaches on the trolley, the three woke as if from a dream. A look of relief spread across their faces. The old lady looked kindly at the confused restocking girl. The old man removed his cap and rubbed his head hard from back to front. The toddler giggled and waved his arms. Ms. Leopard raised an eyebrow, cheeks flushed, and freed one hand to unfasten two buttons of her fur coat.

They smiled at each other.

The old lady finally picked a can, weighed it in her hand, and left with satisfaction. The old man selected another and immediately began explaining to the air that this one was clearly better than the others. Ms. Leopard put two into her cart. After a brief hesitation, she added a third.

The three carts rolled away in different directions, turning left and right through the bright red decorations until they finally disappeared.

By the time the music changed again, the shelf was quiet. After ten seconds of silence, a smooth saxophone began to play.
